# Example Weekly GM Brief

This is a sample output from the Weekly GM Brief workflow, synthesizing a simulated week of nightly logbook entries (November 4–10, 2024). It demonstrates the five-section format and the type of synthesis the LLM produces from accumulated shift data.

### Weekly Snapshot
- **Shifts reviewed:** 10 (5 AM, 5 PM across 5 operating days; weekend included Sat/Sun AM)
- **Total weekly sales:** $89,400 — a **strong week**, approximately 12% above the trailing four-week average
- **Strongest shift:** Friday PM — $26,200; driven by two large pre-booked parties and sustained bar volume
- **Weakest shift:** Monday PM — $7,100; quiet weeknight, below-average volume, no operational concerns

---

### Recurring Operational Themes

The following themes appeared across **3 or more shifts** this week:

- **Expo understaffing at peak volume** *(4 of 10 shifts)*: Expo was flagged as a bottleneck on Saturday AM, Friday PM, and two other mid-week PM shifts with above-average covers. In each case, one expo person was handling ticket volume that pushed throughput delays to the kitchen-to-floor handoff. This is the most consistent operational friction point of the week.

- **Host coverage gaps on medium-volume evenings** *(3 of 10 shifts)*: Single-host configuration on Wednesday PM, Thursday PM, and Sunday PM was flagged as insufficient by shift leads. The pattern suggests the one-host default is calibrated for slow nights and doesn't scale to a 7pm+ rush without a second person.

- **Kitchen allergy modification protocol** *(2 of 10 shifts)*: An allergy modification was missed by the kitchen on Wednesday PM (second occurrence this month) and a near-miss was noted on Thursday AM. Expo caught both. The protocol is not being consistently followed at the kitchen level.

---

### Staffing Observations

- **Servers and bartenders:** Appropriate across the week; no consistent over- or under-staffing signals. Friday PM's 9-server floor was justified by the large-party volume.
- **Expo:** Chronically understaffed on shifts with >$10,000 in revenue. One expo is adequate below that threshold; two should be standard above it.
- **Hosts:** The one-host default works on AM shifts and slow weeknights. It breaks down on PM shifts above ~$9,000 in revenue. A simple volume-based host scheduling rule would address this.
- **AM vs PM:** AM shifts ran leaner overall and flagged fewer staffing concerns; PM shifts accounted for all but one of the staffing flags this week.

### Unresolved Items & GM Recommendations

**Unresolved items carried across multiple shifts:**

- **Allergy modification kitchen miss** — occurred twice this month (Wednesday Nov 6 and a near-miss Thursday Nov 7). Expo has caught both. Not yet addressed at the kitchen-management level. Escalation was requested by shift leads on both nights.
- **POS terminal, Section 3** — went down Wednesday PM; IT ticket submitted but no confirmation of resolution noted in Thursday or Friday logs. Confirm status before Monday open.
- **Large-party dietary capture process** — flagged on Friday PM after the 16-top corporate group's requirements were not in the reservation system. No formal process change has been initiated.

**Recommendations for the weekly meeting:**

1. **Address the kitchen allergy protocol directly with the kitchen manager.** Two misses in one month is a pattern, not a fluke. The expo catching these is not a sustainable safeguard. Agenda item: what does the verification step look like before a modified plate leaves the pass?

2. **Approve two-expo staffing as the standard for shifts forecasted above $10,000.** The data this week is consistent: one expo person is a bottleneck above that threshold. This is a low-cost scheduling change with a direct service quality impact. Bring the schedule template to the meeting.

3. **Create a large-party intake checklist for the host/reservations team.** Friday's 16-top had undocumented dietary needs that reached the floor unannounced. A simple pre-shift communication step (reservations emails the kitchen 24h out for any party of 8+) would prevent this. Assign an owner and a target date.
